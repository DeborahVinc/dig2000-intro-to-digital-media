Tribute to Mother Teresa

BACKGROUND

Born in Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu in Uskup, Ottoman Empire (now Skopje, North Macedonia) on Aug 26, 1910. She is of Albanian descent. At 12 inspired by Missionaries in Bengal, comminted to religious life. At 18 joined Sisters of Loreto in Ireland, to learn English to be a missionary herself, and soon becoming a Roman Catholic Nun. At 1929 arrived at India, learning Bengali and teaching. In 1946 Mother Teresa felt a call to serve the poor in India, and from then on till the end of her life she served the poor in India.

ACHIEVEMENTS

  • In 1950, she founded the Missionaries of Charity, to help "the hungry, the naked, the homeless, the crippled, the blind" in India.
  • She also opened first Home for the Dying, called Nirmal Hriday, in Kolkata.
  • The establishment of Shantio Nagar (City of Peace) was made to help those dealing with leprosy.
  • She also created home for abandoned infants and children.
  • She opened the Gift of Peace AIDS Hospice for those that have AIDS nad other diseases.
  • And in 1979 recieving a Nobel Peace Prize.

CHALLENGES

Though she did a lot, she also face a lot of challenges.
For example she faced spiritual dryness for decades, when she felt God's absence like a "dark night of the soul, with great doubts." She also had to face fundraising friticisms, for accepting donations from some and doing questional financial practices. And her opposition to abortion and contraception lead to many critisms. Her focus on proving immedidate aid to the suffering, didn't address the root issues that led to proverty.

QUOTES

Some of the notable quotes from Mother Teresa is:

“In this life we cannot do great things. We can only do small things with great love.”

 “It is not the magnitude of our actions but the amount of love that is put into them that matters.”

 “We will be the happiest people in the world if we belong to God, if we place ourselves at His disposal, if we let Him use us as He pleases.”

 “There is always the danger that we may just do the work for the sake of the work. This is where the respect and the love and the devotion come in – that we do it to God, to Christ, and that’s why we try to do it as beautifully as possible.”

3 FUN FACTS

  1. Though she is born in August 26 of of 1910, she says August 27 as here birthday as that's when she was baptized.
  2. She has gotten a citizen in India, though she was born in Skopje, North Macedonia.
  3. Though Mother Teresa won the Nobel Peace Prince, she refused the Nobel Peace Prize banquet and asked that the money be donated to the poor.

Here is a brief timeline of Mother Teresa's Life:

Dates Events
August 26, 1910 Born in Skopje, North Macedonia.
1922-1923 First Call to Religious Life.
September 25, 1928 Became a Roman Catholic Nun (Sister Teresa).
1929 Becomes a Teacher After Moving to Kolkata.
1937 Takes Final Vows of a Nun, Becomes Mother Teresa.
1946 Call to serve the poor ("call within a call").
October 7, 1950 Founded Missionaries of Charity.
1957 Begins Work with Lepers.
1971 Receives Pope John XXIII Peace Prize.
1979 Wins Nobel Peace Prize.
March, 1980 Awarded with the Bharat Ratna.
1982 Rescues 37 Disabled Children.
1985 Receives Medal of Freedom (Highest US Honor).
September 5, 1997 Died in Calcutta.
September 4, 2016 Canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta.
